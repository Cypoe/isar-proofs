The Ontological Narrative

1. The Ontological Substrate

At the bottom of the stack lies the Tensor Space ($T$): a representation-free ontological substrate. Objects are compared purely via extensional equivalence ($ExtEq$), avoiding syntactic bias. It is populated by primitive operators corresponding to combinators $I, K, W, C, B$. The $S$ combinator is not primitive; it is derived constructively and proved to extensionally satisfy the $S$-reduction rule.

2. The Invariant Layer

Above the substrate is the Invariant Layer, obtained by taking the quotient of the symbolic calculus modulo operational equivalence. We prove that this forms a rigorous equivalence relation. For any term possessing a normal form, there exists a unique canonical representative, establishing that the invariant layer packages computation independent of concrete evaluation paths.

3. Epistemic Pluralism

Under Epistemic View Pluralism, different formalisms—such as the lambda calculus, term rewriting systems, stack-based bytecode machines, and hereditarily finite set theory—are not competing foundations. Instead, they are all semantic views (decoders) that project and materialize meaning from the same representation-free operational quotient.

The Epistemological Paradox: CIC vs. ISAR

To verify the universal substrate, we employ Lean 4, a proof assistant based on the Calculus of Inductive Constructions (CIC). This introduces a subtle paradox: we use a typed, terminating metalanguage (Lean) to prove properties of a Turing-complete, potentially diverging object language (ISAR). We resolve this by decoupling execution from reasoning. Lean does not run infinite computations; it reasons statically over inductive step relations, allowing terminating logic to certify general-recursive dynamics.

The Gauge Shadow: Category-theoretic terminality demands that any two matrix models ($K_1$, $K_2$) of the substrate must be isomorphic. At the carrier matrix layer, this algebraic isomorphism manifests precisely as a gauge similarity transformation: $P \cdot K_1 \cdot P^{-1} = K_2$. The gauge-dependence represents our choice of coordinates (the matrix entries), while the gauge-invariance represents the terminal semantics.
